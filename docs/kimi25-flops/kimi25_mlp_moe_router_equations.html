Kimi K2.5 MLP, MoE, and Router FLOP Equations

This note is the equation-style companion to section 4.1 Raw equations for Dense MLP, MoE, and router in kimi25_linear_flops_constant.ipynb.

The goal is to separate three ideas:

  1. The first transformer layer uses a normal dense SwiGLU MLP.
  1. The remaining MoE layers use a router to choose experts.
  1. Only the selected experts run their MLPs, which is the sparse-MoE saving.

Throughout this note, one matrix multiply from shape [a] -> [b] costs:

$$ 2ab $$

The factor 2 is multiply plus add.

1. Dense Layer 0: Ordinary SwiGLU MLP

Layer 0 is not MoE. It uses a dense SwiGLU MLP.

For token hidden state:

$$ x_t \in \mathbb{R}^{H} $$

the dense MLP computes:

$$ g_t = x_t W_{\text{gate}} $$

$$ u_t = x_t W_{\text{up}} $$

$$ m_t = \operatorname{silu}(g_t) \odot u_t $$

$$ y_t = m_t W_{\text{down}} $$

where:

$$ W_{\text{gate}} \in \mathbb{R}^{H \times dense\_intermediate} $$

$$ W_{\text{up}} \in \mathbb{R}^{H \times dense\_intermediate} $$

$$ W_{\text{down}} \in \mathbb{R}^{dense\_intermediate \times H} $$

So the matmul FLOPs are:

$$ dense\_mlp\_layer = 2H \cdot dense\_intermediate + 2H \cdot dense\_intermediate + 2dense\_intermediate \cdot H $$

Therefore:

$$ dense\_mlp\_layer = 6H \cdot dense\_intermediate $$

For Kimi K2.5:

$$ dense\_mlp\_layer = 6 \cdot 7168 \cdot 18432 = 792{,}723{,}456 $$

2. MoE Router: Score All Routed Experts

In each MoE layer, the router first scores all candidate routed experts:

$$ router\_logits_t = x_t W_{\text{router}} $$

where:

$$ W_{\text{router}} \in \mathbb{R}^{H \times routed\_experts} $$

Then the model chooses the top routed experts:

$$ selected_t = \operatorname{topk}(router\_logits_t, k=8) $$

The router matmul FLOPs are:

$$ router\_per\_moe\_layer = 2H \cdot routed\_experts $$

For Kimi K2.5:

$$ router\_per\_moe\_layer = 2 \cdot 7168 \cdot 384 = 5{,}505{,}024 $$

The router scores all 384 routed experts, but scoring is much cheaper than running all expert MLPs.

3. One Active Expert: SwiGLU MLP

For one selected expert e, the expert itself is also a SwiGLU MLP:

$$ g_{t,e} = x_t W_{\text{gate},e} $$

$$ u_{t,e} = x_t W_{\text{up},e} $$

$$ m_{t,e} = \operatorname{silu}(g_{t,e}) \odot u_{t,e} $$

$$ y_{t,e} = m_{t,e} W_{\text{down},e} $$

where:

$$ W_{\text{gate},e} \in \mathbb{R}^{H \times moe\_intermediate} $$

$$ W_{\text{up},e} \in \mathbb{R}^{H \times moe\_intermediate} $$

$$ W_{\text{down},e} \in \mathbb{R}^{moe\_intermediate \times H} $$

The FLOPs for one active expert are:

$$ one\_expert = 2H \cdot moe\_intermediate + 2H \cdot moe\_intermediate + 2moe\_intermediate \cdot H $$

Therefore:

$$ one\_expert = 6H \cdot moe\_intermediate $$

For Kimi K2.5:

$$ one\_expert = 6 \cdot 7168 \cdot 2048 = 88{,}080{,}384 $$

4. Active Experts Per Token

Kimi K2.5 uses:

$$ topk = 8 $$

$$ shared\_experts = 1 $$

So the number of experts that actually run per token is:

$$ active\_experts = topk + shared\_experts = 9 $$

The active expert FLOPs for one token in one MoE layer are:

$$ active\_moe\_layer = active\_experts \cdot one\_expert $$

Substitute the expert formula:

$$ active\_moe\_layer = active\_experts \cdot 6H \cdot moe\_intermediate $$

For Kimi K2.5:

$$ active\_moe\_layer = 9 \cdot 6 \cdot 7168 \cdot 2048 = 792{,}723{,}456 $$

5. Why This Does Not Count All 384 Experts

The router computes:

$$ router\_logits_t \in \mathbb{R}^{384} $$

but the model does not run:

$$ 384 \cdot one\_expert $$

Instead, it runs:

$$ 9 \cdot one\_expert $$

That is the core sparse-MoE saving:

$$ \frac{9}{384} = 2.34\% $$

So each MoE layer pays a small router cost plus the cost of 9 active experts, not 384 full experts.
