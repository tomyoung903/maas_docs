Precision-aware MFU

The numerator counts Tensor-Core work in H200/B300 FP8-equivalent units. FP8 MMA is ×1; proven BF16/FP16 MMA is ×2; scalar, SFU, and unresolved work is not converted. FP32 accumulation inside an FP8 or BF16 Tensor-Core instruction does not turn the entire GEMM into a separate FP32-throughput workload.

B300 canonical warm baseline The captured TRT-LLM sparse-attention symbol proves E4M3 Q/K/V, so the B300 numerator stays at the FP8 logical work value.
H200 normalized companion H200’s BF16 FlashMLA attention contributes one additional raw-attention amount when normalized ×2. This is a comparability convention, not extra executed model work.

Why it wins CUTLASS saves in feed-forward while attention + selector regresses by . The net PP2 saving is , and eight repetitions of that representative PP2 saving explain about 97.69% of the full-request median improvement.
Why FlashMLA was not timed here Both FlashMLA candidates failed the exact B300 FP8-KV dtype contract before a valid latency observation. Changing KV construction or cache dtype would be a different experiment with possible precision and memory consequences.
